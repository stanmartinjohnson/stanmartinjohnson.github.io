Research Projects

The Digital Sallust Archive
A comprehensive digital edition of Sallust's works with advanced search capabilities, linguistic analysis tools, and intertextual connections to contemporary sources. This project aims to provide scholars with new ways to analyze rhetorical patterns and historical methodologies in Late Republican historiography.
Status: In development (funded by NEH Digital Humanities Start-Up Grant)
Civil War Narratives in Ancient Historiography
Comparative study examining how different ancient historians (Sallust, Livy, Tacitus, Appian) constructed narratives of civil conflict. Focus on rhetorical strategies, moral frameworks, and political implications of their historical accounts.
Status: Manuscript in preparation
Women in Roman Historical Texts
Analysis of the representation of women in Roman historiography, examining how female characters function within broader narratives of political and social change. Particular attention to the intersection of gender, class, and political agency.
Status: Early research phase
